## Context

Two residual precision gaps after D-3-A + D-3-B:

1. **Narrowing-produces-empty-result** (p1-p2-hybrid case): docs like TC 3-22.240 detect `section_structure='both'` but Section II narrowing yields no usable range; parser emits 0 entries even though the doc has Section I acronyms parseable in the full range.
2. **Lowercase-stopword noise**: under some configurations the parser admits `the`, `there`, etc. as terms when the X-only fallback or column-gate over-admits. These are not real glossary headwords.

## Approach (two small additions)

### D-3-C-1: Narrowed-range-empty-result fallback
After `parse_glossary_entries` runs with `section_ii_header_pattern` set, if it returns 0 entries AND `section_structure` is `both` or `section_ii_only`, re-invoke `parse_glossary_entries` with `section_ii_header_pattern=None` over the FULL `[start, end]` range (skip narrowing). Implemented in `core/analyzer.py` post-narrowed-parse logic.

### D-3-C-2: Lowercase-stopword rejection in `_validate_term`
Extend `_validate_term` to reject terms that are `lowercase AND len < 5 AND in {the, there, this, these, that, when, who, how, why, what, with, from, into}`. This is a small stopword list, not a full English-stopwords list — only the cases empirically observed in D-3-B's noise output.
